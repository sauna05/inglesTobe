Verb To Be

What is the Verb To Be

The verb "to be" is one of the most important and versatile verbs in English. It translates to "ser" or "estar" in Spanish and is used in a variety of situations to describe states, identities, locations, and more.

Forms of the Verb To Be

Depending on the subject, the verb "to be" changes its form. Here are its three main conjugations in the present tense:

Subject Form of verb to be Example
I Am I am happy
You Are you are my friend
He/She/It Is She is a teacher
We Are We are at the park
They Are They are students

Uses of the Verb To Be

Identity

To say who you are or who someone else is.

I am a student. He is a doctor.

Emotional or Physical States

To describe how you feel or the state you are in.

She is tired. We are happy.

Location

To sa where you are or where something is.

You are in the  classroom. The keys are on the  table

Charateristics

To describe characteristics of people, places or things.

The sky is blue They are tall.

Questions and Negations with To Be

To Ask Questions

You just have to reverse the order of the subject and the verb. Affirmative: you are ready. Question: Are you ready?

Negative Form

Add "not" after the verb.

Affirmative: I am tired Negative: I am NOT tired

Examples

Subject Affirmative Negative Questions
I I am a student. I am not a student. Am I a student?
You Am I a student? You are not happy. Are you happy?
He He is a teacher. He is not a teacher. Is he a teacher?
She She is at home. She is not at home. Is she at home?
It It is a book. It is not a book. Is it a book?
We We are friends. We are not friends. Are we friends?
They They are ready. They are not ready. Are they ready?

Contractions

In English, it is common to use contractions to make speech and writing faster and more fluid.

The verb "to be" can be contracted with the subject, as shown below:

Normal Contraction
I am I'm
You are You're
He/She/It is He's / She's / It's
We are We're
They are They're
